About

I created my first program at age 10. It was a Windows form in Visual Basic 6 that, when clicked, would move to a different location on the screen. It was a simple program, but I learned that with the right skills, I could make computers do anything I wanted. The rest of my life has been an adventure in learning new ways to do that.

Now I spend most of my free time working on those skills. I maintain a "home lab" with servers and varied computers that I practice on to learn new skills, mainly in Windows Active Directory. I frequently create small projects as well as contribute to open source projects to keep my programming skills fresh.

When I'm not glued to the computer, I like to ski, SCUBA, and mix cocktails. I volunteer with New Century Hospice, providing companionship and emotional support to terminally ill patients. I experiment with HAM radio, like the below picture where I helped launch a weather balloon for K5UTD in August 2014.

Crescent Moon Proposal

Crescent Moon is a small, family-owned restaurant in Plano that serves a mostly Muslim clientele. The owners, who are Muslim, follow Islamic guidelines regarding Halal foods, and use special meats and meet special preparation requirements. The business had trouble expanding, as its customer base was already largely expanded. I lead a team of six fellow students to research the marketing position of the company and come up with ways to increase their customer base. I was the lead speaker in delivering the 30-minute presentation to the class and the owners, who were special guests. I have added a few slides with information on the effects of our recommendations as well as what I would recommend be done in addition.
